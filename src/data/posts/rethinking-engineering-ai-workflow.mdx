## Thesis (TBD)

- Engineering is converging on **specification → agentic execution → human review → automated verification** — not replacing craft, but relocating where judgment sits.
- The interesting design space is not “prompt vs. IDE” but **contracts**: what must stay true, what can vary, and how we prove it.

## Sections to develop

1. **From tickets to intent** — requirements as durable artifacts (not throwaway JIRA text) that machines and people can both reason about.
2. **The review surface** — diffs, tests, and traces as the primary UI of trust; speed without auditability is debt.
3. **Simplifying structure** — fewer bespoke layers, clearer boundaries; AI favors flat, well-named seams over clever indirection.
4. **What “senior” means now** — architecture as curating invariants and failure modes, not memorizing APIs.

## Open questions

- Where does liability and ownership land when automation is default?
- How do we teach this without flattening engineering into “chat ops”?
